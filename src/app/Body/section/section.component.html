ARE THE SYMPTOMS OF COVID-19?

SYMPTOMS OF CORONAVIRUS

People with COVID-19 have had a wide range of symptoms reported ranging from mild symptoms to severe illness. Symptoms may appear 2-14 days after exposure to the virus. Anyone can have mild to severe symptoms.

People with these symptoms may have COVID-19:

  • Fatigue
  • Cough
  • Headache
  • Fever or chills
  • Muscle or body aches
  • Congestion or runny nose
  • New loss of taste or smell
  • Nausea or vomiting

    • Influenza (Flu) and COVID-19 are both contagious respiratory illnesses,
    but they are caused by different viruses.COVID-19 is caused by infection with a new coronavirus (called SARS-CoV-2), and flu is caused by infection with influenza viruses. COVID-19 seems to spread more easily than flu and causes more serious illnesses in some people.
    It can also take longer before people show symptoms and people can be contagious for longer.
